The green mission from Plantify

Plantify originated from the will to literally green houses with the most beautiful houseplants. Buying fresh house plants could be simpler and more convenient instead of lifting a fracture in the garden center, you had to be able to do it online to order. But, not only our plants need the most beautiful shade of green we also go for green in our business processes. Which means; less use of plastic and more focus on recycling, CO2 emissions compensation, a longer life of your houseplant, a transparent production process and cooperate with "green" initiatives.

A long and green plant life

At Plantify, we think it is important that the houseplant you purchase has a long life. That is not only good for your wallet, because you don't have to buy a new houseplant as often, but it is also less harmful to the environment! If we all keep our houseplants alive longer, we can reduce the impact of polluting production processes!

Delivery without worries!

Unfortunately, just like driving to the garden center, delivering parcels entails CO2 emissions. But, because we want to minimize our impact on the planet, we work together with USPS. They are working hard to deliver completely emission-free in 25 inner cities from 2025, with bicycles and electric vehicles. In addition, we are looking into the possibilities of using a tool that calculates the CO2 emissions per package. With these insights, we can not only optimize our delivery method, but also investigate how we can best offset those emissions.

Less plastic and more efficiency

Our packaging is made from FSC cardboard and is therefore produced as sustainably as possible. We ship houseplants in all kinds of different shapes and sizes, which means there is sometimes still empty space in the box. Currently we use 4 sizes of boxes, but if we want to use a box that fits the plant perfectly for all plants, we need 10 to 15 different box sizes. That challenge is still too great. We are, however, developing a box that can hold several plants at the same time! We are also trying to develop an adaptable box. In addition, we have set ourselves the goal of no longer using plastic to protect the plant in the box by the end of 2022. We would also like to replace the protective plastic for the ceramic pots with a sustainable method!
